### World Lore: The Second Sundering and The Collapse

**The Death of Mystra and the Spellplague**
* **The Cataclysm:** The world's current state is a direct result of the death of Mystra, the Goddess of Magic. Her demise triggered the "Spellplague," a period where the Weave (the source of all magic) was shredded. 
* **The Broken Weave:** Magic became unpredictable and "living," manifesting as blue flames that mutated creatures and warped the landscape. This resulted in "Earthmotes" (islands of land floating in the sky) and the permanent alteration of many coastal regions.
* **The Silent Deities:** Following the death of Mystra and the subsequent chaos, many gods became distant or "silent," leading to a rise in secular power and the emergence of desperate new cults promising order through dark means.

### The Tyranny of Dragons (1487 - 1492 DR)

**The Five-Year War**
* **The Rise of Tiamat:** A cult dedicated to Tiamat, the Queen of Evil Dragons, attempted to summon her physically into the Material Plane. This sparked a global conflict that consumed the Sword Coast for five years.
* **Living Nukes:** Dragons were utilized as "living nukes," laying waste to major cities and farmland. The sheer scale of destruction led to a massive refugee crisis that defines the current demographics of Waterdeep.
* **The Final Assault:** The war culminated at the Well of Dragons. The conflict was fought on multiple fronts, including the "Northern Assault" (involving elven ranger battalions) and the "Southern Assault" (involving mercenary and holy orders). 

***

### The Fall of the First Company

**The Mystery of Captain Gur Malla**
* **The Commander:** Captain Gur Malla was a legendary figure and mentor who was supposed to lead the unified forces at the Well of Dragons.
* **The Disappearance:** On the eve of the final battle, Captain Malla vanished without a trace. This disappearance caused a total collapse in the chain of command.
* **The Consequences:** Without Malla's leadership, the Northern and Southern assaults were uncoordinated. Reinforcements never arrived for the North, and the Southern mercenary lines were thrown into a "Southern Collapse." This event is the shared trauma that binds the current party together, as they were the survivors of the units Malla was supposed to lead.

### The Field Ward History

**The Unofficial Ward**
* **Origin:** Originally just a muddy area outside the North Gate where caravans parked, it became a permanent fixture during the dragon wars. 
* **Refugee Hub:** As the dragons razed the surrounding countryside and smaller towns like Amphail and Rassalvan, thousands fled to Waterdeep. The city refused to open the main gates to everyone, forcing them to settle in the "Field Ward" between the inner and outer walls.
* **Sovereignty:** Because it is technically outside the legal city limits of the inner wall, the City Watch provides no protection there, leaving the area to be ruled by street gangs and local "spokespeople."

***

### The Political Shift of Waterdeep

**The Ousting of Dagult Neverember**
* **Corruption:** During the war, Open Lord Dagult Neverember focused heavily on rebuilding Neverwinter (his ancestral home) using Waterdeep’s tax gold.
* **The Exile:** In 1492 DR, shortly before the game began, the Masked Lords discovered his embezzlement and ousted him. He was replaced by Laeral Silverhand.
* **The Power Vacuum:** Neverember’s departure left the city in financial strain and created a vacuum in the underworld, allowing the Xanathar Guild and the Zhentarim to grow bolder in their efforts to control the city's remaining wealth.
